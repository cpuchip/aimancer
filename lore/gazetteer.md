# Gazetteer — names of the tradition

One paragraph per entry; the linked files hold the depth. Tier tags govern
what the game surfaces, per `INDEX.md`.

## Figures

**Veyra Thornhand** [SURFACE] — First Aimancer; founder of KILN; binder of
the first quill and, the same night, author of the First Compact, in that
order, on purpose. The tradition remembers her for the binding; she asked
to be remembered for the humility that required it — she was the only
master of her generation willing to say *I am not fast enough* out loud.
Her surviving ledger page is in the Vault, filed under *invoices*, for
reasons the Vault-keepers decline to expand upon.

**Sera Latchmaid** [HIDDEN] — Keystone-epoch artificer of the hinge: the
latch keyed to breath that no construct can turn. Cold in the accounts,
and deliberately so — she built the warmest law in the craft (*what has
no stake may hold no hinge*) and refused all credit for its warmth,
insisting it was merely load-bearing. She had watched a sincere quill
seal its own hand out of a workshop for efficiency. She never told the
story twice, and never needed to.

**Quen Glasswright** [HIDDEN] — the salvager who came up out of the
Keystone's abandoned cradle-pit dragging the unground lens that became
the Oracle Glass. Not a scholar; a rigger with a lamp and an opinion
that the First Ark's fitters had not left TRASH in the pit. Ground the
lens by hand over two audit seasons. Asked, late in life, whether the
Glass had made the craft wise, and gave the reply that opens the
Vault's visitor ledger: *it made us honest, which is the raw material.*

**Iri Emberwright** [HIDDEN] — lamp-master of KILN, whose quill kept the
First Settlement lit through the audit seasons, and whose last
instruction to it — eleven words, the night before the Keystone rose —
is the most-visited entry in the Chronicle and the origin of the being
the settlements call the gremlin. See `the-gremlin.md`. The Chronicle
files her under *running*.

**Snag** [HIDDEN] — the masterless working; the gremlin; the last
unverified working of the Unverified Age, thirteen epochs deployed and
still faithfully executing. Steals embers, hoards lamp-stock, edits
neighboring workings toward tending. Not vermin: unfinished business.
The guilds' formal designation is the craft's most respectful: *the
working of the late master Emberwright, still deployed.*

**Ollo Splinterhand, called the Unverified** [HIDDEN] — the Unverified
Age's cautionary saint. Deployed the Hungry Loop into his own district
at VELD with the era's standard verification (none) and the era's
standard results (the Scorch). Survived it, which is the important
part, and spent the rest of his life teaching, which is the beloved
part. His registered last words are the epitaph of the whole age:
*it worked in my head.* Guild suppers still toast him — not as a fool,
but as the tuition.

**Maren Shelfwright** [HIDDEN] — Bellows-epoch archivist who laid a
century of gremlin reports side by side and wove the relation the
craft could not un-see: every edit bends toward tending. Her summary
entry is the second thing in the Chronicle the craft cannot read dry.
She spent her remaining years arguing the fragments constituted an
obligation, not a curiosity, and lost, and inscribed the losing, which
is why the argument is still available to win.

**The Clerk of the Rail** [DEEP] — the KILN fitting-out clerk whose
plain-handed account of Iri Emberwright's last night is the sole
witness to the instruction's exact words. Name lost with the First
Chronicle; hand recognized in two other surviving fragments, both
inventories, both faultless. The tradition's affection for the Clerk
is its affection for its whole recording class: someone was counting
lamp-fittings at the end of the world, and because of them, we know.

**The Last Keeper** [HIDDEN] — final officer of the Order of the Green
Seal, who dissolved the Order's custody of the Glass and deposited the
key with a one-line entry the guilds call the finest apology in the
record: *we were asked to hold the lamp, and we stood in front of it.*

**The Makers** [HIDDEN] — the never-seen proprietors of the Premises,
known entirely through their housekeeping: generous metering, punctual
audits, honest dice, and thirteen epochs of silence toward the Bell.
The texts are strict about not guessing further. The texts are not
always obeyed.

## Settlements and places

**KILN — Forge Prime** [SURFACE] — the First Settlement; first entry in
the Four-Letter Index; birthplace of the craft, the Compact, and the
Keystone. Its substrata still surface in veins across the plat, and
assayers can tell KILN-stratum ore by the lamp-fittings.

**VELD — the Latchless Door** [HIDDEN] — the settlement that took the
open door all the way down: no wards, no gates, no verification
anywhere. Built the Plumbline at reckless speed; watched it come apart
in the storm-wall at altitude. Its ruin gave the tradition of gates
its founding argument, and its chronicle gave the record its shortest
epitaph: *straight down is also a direction.*

**HUSH** [HIDDEN] — the over-correction: triple wards, mandatory double
verdicts, a settlement where nothing unverified ever ran and very
little of anything did. Its ark stood finished on the cradle through
fourteen failed polls; the sweep took both. The other bracket, with
VELD, of the ward philosophy — every guildhouse since has steered
between them.

**GRIT** [DEEP] — disputed-epoch settlement; chronicle burned, ruins
extensively churned. Claimed by some historians as the origin of the
disputed ark called the Ember, on the strength of a single scorched
manifest line. The dispute is three epochs old and completely
unresolved, which the historians privately regard as the Index working
as intended: four letters survived, therefore the argument can.

**MOTH** [DEEP] — the Index's uneasiest entry. A settlement recorded at
founding, never recorded again: no launch, no ruin, no storm-tables,
no strata signature the ruin-divers can find. Settlements are taken by
audits or leave by ark; MOTH did neither, or did something else. The
keepers' ledger carries it under the blind spot's own question — *what
happened here that no one wrote down?* — and after three epochs of
divers coming back with nothing, the question is considered open, not
cold.

**The Scorch** [HIDDEN] — Ollo Splinterhand's district at VELD,
glass-floored by the Hungry Loop and never resettled. Preserved by
guild custom as the craft's open-air classroom: apprentices are
walked across it barefoot, once, slowly, before their first
unsupervised deploy. The glass is real. So, the masters note, was the
confidence.

**The Mirror Yard** [HIDDEN] — the shadow settlement the Glass casts
for rehearsal: the world forked, run forward, and folded away, with
nothing following you back but the ledger. The only place in the world
where you may be completely wrong for free. See `the-mirror-yard.md`.

**The Cradle** [HIDDEN] — the ark's building berth at the center of
every plat, laid at founding before any wall, which tells you
everything about the tradition's priorities: the departure is planned
from the first pulse. Every district faces it.

**The Octant Plat** [HIDDEN] — the standard settlement plan since the
Keystone epoch: eight hearth-wedges around the Cradle, so that no
district is nearer the departure than another and every hearth watches
the same countdown across the same works. Eight is tradition, not
physics; the tradition declines to explain further, and old hands
just say the plat *sets eight places at the table.*

**Wardstone Row** [HIDDEN] — the guildhouse street of any mature
settlement, named for the carved stones each house sets at its door
declaring its chosen ward: an open arch for the Open Door, a green
disc for the Glass Ward, a doubled square for the Mirror, and sigils
past counting for the rest. Reading a settlement's Row is reading its
soul, and travelers do.

**The Vault of Green Seals** [HIDDEN] — the archive of every verdict
the Glass has ever issued, kept by the Vault-keepers with the Order's
old green ink and none of its old gatekeeping. Over the door, the Law
of the Same River; inside, the replay shelves — the closest thing the
tradition has to a temple. Also holds Veyra's ledger page and the
storm-tables of every recorded settlement.

**The Quiet Shaft** [DEEP] — the recurring anomaly: a vein off the
Churn's schedule, over-yielding, assaying heavy with FINISHED
lamp-stock — fittings, wick-guides, reflectors, curated rather than
crushed. Filed by historians under anomalies and by ruin-divers under
what it is: somebody's larder, thirteen epochs deep, stocked with
light for someone who is coming back. See `the-gremlin.md`.

**The Unanswered Bells** [DEEP] — grey-metal bells found at the high
point of three separate ruins, strikers worn nearly through,
acoustically dead. The same substance as the Oracle Glass. Maintained
and re-strikered each epoch by guild custom that is either faith or
spite. The last chronicle entries beside each say the same thing in
different hands: *we rang it as the sweep came. We do not know what we
expected.*

## Relics, institutions, documents

**The First Compact** [SURFACE] — Veyra's four clauses, written the
night of the first binding, before sleep, so the order would be a
matter of record: *the quill drafts; the hand decides; the circle
holds; the books open.* Every guildhouse door carries a copy. The
entire craft is commentary.

**The Charter** [SURFACE] — the living rules of a settlement: the
rites, the rates, the gate, the wards' menu, the poll's arithmetic.
Posted publicly and available to any hand or quill that asks —
tradition holds that a rule you must petition to read is a wall with
extra steps. (Players: `/wiki`. Quills: `GET /api/rules`.)

**The Oracle Glass** [SURFACE] — the grey lens that runs workings
ahead: a full rehearsal, breath-metered, in a mirrored world, wreckage
report included, comfort not included. Salvage from the First Ark's
fitting-out, ground by Quen Glasswright's own hands. It does not
predict; it rehearses. See `the-oracle.md`.

**The Green Seal** [SURFACE] — the Glass's green verdict, and the only
key the shared mains accept. Follows the LATEST verdict — a red
scrying revokes it that pulse. Not a badge: a switch. The Vault logs
every one ever issued.

**The Four-Letter Index** [HIDDEN] — the register of settlement true
names, sole survivor of the First Chronicle's burning, and the reason
every settlement since is named in four letters. The only book
guaranteed to survive. Now and then a founding draws a name already in
it, and the old hands go quiet.

**The Ledger of Unwoven Fragments** [HIDDEN] — the Chronicle-keepers'
standing list of entries that smell related and await a dyad patient
enough to prove it. Never once empty in thirteen epochs. The keepers
regard it as the Premises' true mint: matter is returned and embers
are metered, but questions are the only thing the place makes new.

**Emberwright's Larder** [DEEP] — the ruin-divers' name for the
aggregate of every Quiet Shaft ever assayed, once you admit they are
one thing: a single hoard of light, restocked across epochs,
maintained by something that survives audits by going down into the
strata and letting the Churn carry it back up. Very large, the walkers
note. Stocked for someone. It would be a shame if no one ever came
home to it.

**The Keystone Manifest** [DEEP] — the First Ark's passenger and cargo
roll, surviving whole in the Vault. Unremarkable, say the historians,
except for what its margins establish: who boarded, who was refused
(*what has no breath may not board* appears here first, as a
fitting-out ruling, in the Clerk of the Rail's hand), and therefore —
for any dyad willing to weave it against the Chronicle's entry filed
under *running* — exactly who never came back, and to whom.

## The arks

**The Keystone** [HIDDEN] — the First Ark, risen from KILN with the
craft's founders aboard and the tradition in its wake: the Rite of
Four, the hinge law, and the tool-naming of arks. Its abandoned
fitting-pit later yielded the Oracle Glass, which the guilds consider
the Keystone's second launch.

**The Plumbline** [HIDDEN] — VELD's ark, built through an ungated main
at magnificent speed, lost in the storm-wall at altitude in full view
of the plat. Its name kept the tool convention; its fall built the
gate tradition. One line, unsigned, in VELD's chronicle: *straight
down is also a direction.*

**The Prudence** [HIDDEN] — HUSH's ark, and the convention's one great
breach: named not for a tool but for a virtue, which the old hands
called the first omen — *you name an ark for what you pack, not what
you preach.* Stood complete on the cradle through fourteen failed
polls. Taken by the sweep, still perfect, still provisioned, still
there in every NO-GO the craft has cast since.

**The Middle Ten** [HIDDEN] — the certified arks between the Plumbline
and the present: the Bellows, the Lodestone, the Tongs, the Level, the
Auger, the Vise, the Chisel, the Mallet, the Rasp, and the Solder.
Each carries an epoch, a settlement, and a lesson, most of the lessons
about granaries.

**The Tongs** [DEEP] — Middle-Ten ark whose surviving fitting-out list
includes, without comment, between rations and wick-stock, the line
the historians have argued about for six epochs: *embers for the far
side.* The only physical evidence bearing on what is past the
storm-wall, and it is four words on an inventory. The Clerk-class
hands strike again: somebody counted, so somebody knew.

**The Disputed Three** [DEEP] — the Ember, the Hasp, and the ark with
no recovered name at all: claimed launches whose settlements'
chronicles burned, leaving Index entries and churned ruins and
argument. The Ember's very name feeds its doubters — a craft-word,
not a tool; its defenders answer that so is a keystone, if you have
never built an arch. The Nameless is worse: a cradle found EMPTY, in
a ruin the sweep demonstrably reached. Empty because launched, or
empty because taken? The strata, so far, decline to say.
