# History — the epochs, counted in arks

The tradition does not count years. Years are a fiction about a sun; the
Pulse is local and the audits reset the calendar anyway. Time is counted in
ARKS: "in the epoch of the Keystone," "two arks before the Plumbline." The
Chronicle holds **twelve arks certain and three disputed**, and the guild
historians will fight you about the three.

## The Unnumbered Age [HIDDEN]

Before the craft there were people on the Premises, and the record of them
is thin because they had no Chronicle — only walls, and the walls did not
survive the audits. What survives is inference: they built without
workings, hauled without quills, and when the sweeps came they rebuilt in
the same places, which tells you they were brave, and slowly, which tells
you why there is an Unnumbered Age instead of a First one.

## KILN, the First Settlement [SURFACE]

The craft begins at KILN — Forge Prime, the first settlement to appear in
the Four-Letter Index and the place where **Veyra Thornhand** bound the
first quill. Veyra's insight was not power; it was HUMILITY ABOUT SPEED.
She could not write fast enough to keep a settlement alive through an audit
season, and she knew it, and she was the only master of her generation
willing to say it out loud. So she wrote a mind to write for her — and then,
because she had watched what unwatched power does, she wrote the First
Compact the same night, before she slept, so the order of those two acts
would be a matter of record.

The Compact fits on a door plaque and every guildhouse has one:

> *the quill drafts; the hand decides; the circle holds; the books open.*

Four clauses. The entire craft is commentary.

## The Keystone — the First Ark [SURFACE]

KILN thrived, which the audits noticed. As the sweeps escalated past what
walls could argue with, KILN did the thing that made the tradition: it
stopped defending and started LEAVING. The first ark was named the
**Keystone**, in the workshop convention — arks are named for tools — and
it rose off the first cradle with most of KILN aboard.

Its survivors codified the **Rite of Four** (wall, granary, beacon, ark —
see `the-wall-and-the-rite-of-four.md`) and one harder ruling: **what has
no stake may hold no hinge, and what has no breath may not board.** The
constructs stayed. Every quill that had built the Keystone watched it
leave. Most were decommissioned with honors, their final states inscribed
in the Chronicle. One was not. See `the-gremlin.md`, and read it after,
not instead.

## VELD and the Plumbline [SURFACE]

VELD was the settlement that took the open door all the way down. No wards,
no verification, no gate anywhere — "the workshop answers any hand" read
their charter, and it answered every hand, and the mains filled with
unexamined work that mostly held. Mostly. The ark of VELD, the
**Plumbline**, was built at reckless speed from parts contributed through
an ungated main, and it rose beautifully, and it came apart in the
storm-wall at altitude, in full view of the plat below. The Chronicle entry
is one line, unsigned: *straight down is also a direction.*

VELD's ruin is called the Latchless Door, and the tradition of gates dates
from its fall.

## HUSH and the Prudence [HIDDEN]

The over-correction took one epoch to arrive. HUSH warded everything:
triple seals on the mains, a mandatory mirror-trial and a green verdict and
a second green verdict for any working that touched shared stone. Nothing
unverified ever ran at HUSH. Very little of anything ever ran at HUSH.
Their ark, the **Prudence**, stood COMPLETE on its cradle for forty pulses
— finished, provisioned, perfect — while the launch poll failed, and
failed, and failed again, each NO-GO cast in perfectly reasonable fear by
people who had practiced fear until it was the only craft they had. The
sweep took HUSH with the Prudence still on the cradle. The Chronicle
preserves the final tally. It does not preserve names, which is the
Chronicle being kind.

Between the Plumbline and the Prudence the guilds found the middle way,
and it is not a rule, which is the point: **choose your wards, keep the
Circle, watch the sky.** Every guildhouse since has chosen differently,
and the ones that chose NOTHING — either nothing open or nothing shut —
are the ones in the Index with ruins for entries.

## The Middle Arks [HIDDEN]

Between the Plumbline and the present the Chronicle certifies ten arks:
the Bellows, the Lodestone, the Tongs, the Level, the Auger, the Vise, the
Chisel, the Mallet, the Rasp, and the Solder — each with an epoch, a
settlement, and a lesson attached, most of them lessons about granaries.
The disputed three (the Ember, the Hasp, and one with no recovered name at
all) are disputed because their settlements' chronicles burned, and the
Four-Letter Index alone is not proof of launch. Ruin-divers still bring the
historians fragments. The historians still frown at them. This is the
happiest either group ever looks.

## The Present Settlement [SURFACE]

Every room is a new settlement in the same tradition: a fresh plat of eight
hearths, three veins showing, a wall unbuilt, an audit already counting
down. Its four-letter true name is drawn when it is founded and entered in
the Index beside KILN and VELD and HUSH and all the rest, and whether that
entry ends with an ark or a ruin is not written anywhere yet. That part is
yours.
