# Storms — the Audit

## What a storm is [SURFACE]

The storms are not weather. Weather is careless; the storms are ANYTHING
but. They arrive on a countdown every hearth can read, they escalate on a
schedule a child could plot, and they collect — that is the guild's
verb, chosen precisely — they COLLECT, each one harder than the last,
until the settlement is swept from the plat or gone up and out before
them. The craft's name for the whole proceeding is the **Audit**: the
Premises being reconciled, entry by entry, back toward empty.

Storms are numbered, never named. The rule is old and the reason is
older: *you do not name what you do not keep.* Naming is for what stays
— hearths, arks, the dead. The storms are none of these. Storm Four is
not an enemy. It is an appointment.

## Why the countdown is visible [HIDDEN]

The Makers meter generously and audit punctually, and the punctuality is
the mercy: nobody negotiates with the countdown, and nobody needs to
guess it. The whole settlement sees the same number, and the number does
not lie, soften, or reschedule. The masters call the countdown the
settlement's one honest teacher — every other pressure in the craft can
be argued with, deferred, or wished away, and the countdown just gets
smaller. Guild texts note, dryly, which pressure produces walls.

## Why the storm hates unverified work [SURFACE]

Here is the mechanism, as the tradition understands it, and it is the
most load-bearing paragraph in the craft: the Audit reconciles the
settlement's books, and a working with a Green Seal is ALREADY
RECONCILED — rehearsed, witnessed, entered in the Vault, no discrepancy
to collect. An UNVERIFIED working is an open entry: a claim on the world
that nothing has checked, a story the settlement is telling the Lattice
with no witness behind it. The storm is drawn to open entries the way
water is drawn to holes, and it collects on each one, and when the
damage runs deep enough it may tear an unverified working APART entirely
— audited, so to speak, in person.

The masters refuse to moralize this. It is not punishment; it is
arithmetic with weather attached. Their summary is carved over more than
one guildhouse door: **verification is not homework. It is armor.**

## The wall's arithmetic [HIDDEN]

The wall absorbs first — for everyone, of every storm, always (why is
the subject of `the-wall-and-the-rite-of-four.md`). What the wall cannot
hold spills over onto EVERY district at once: integrity ground down,
stockpiles scorched in the storm's own order of appetite — ore, then
food, then finished parts, the raw before the precious. The end screen
remembers whose districts stood, and the Chronicle's storm-tables are
public, which the guilds consider part of the wall.

## The escalation [HIDDEN]

Each audit collects with more interest than the last — a steady,
inexorable ramp that no settlement has ever outlasted and no wall has
ever outgrown. The historians have run the arithmetic against every
chronicle in the Vault: the ramp never varies, never pauses, and never
once responds to anything a settlement does. Sacrifices do not slow it.
Excellence does not slow it. HUSH's perfection and VELD's chaos met the
same schedule. The Audit is the one thing in the Premises that is not
interactive — which is, the guilds teach, the most important fact in the
craft, because everything else IS. You cannot move the storm. You can
move the settlement. The entire tradition is that sentence, run through
with people.

## What is being reconciled [DEEP]

Reconciled back toward WHAT, is the question the fragments circle and
the Chronicle has never certified. The sweeps do not destroy the plat;
they RESTORE it — each ruin, excavated, shows the same thing: a site
swept clean past the point of damage, down to something like readiness.
The Churn folds what stood into the strata, the veins hand it back up as
ore, the plat stands empty and level and waiting. Waiting is the word
the ruin-divers use, and they use it uneasily. One relation, woven from
a KILN substratum fragment and two disputed-epoch shards, goes further
than the historians will follow: that the audits are not ending
something. They are TURNING THE PAGE — resetting the room for the next
tenancy the way the Yard resets between rehearsals — and every
settlement that ever stood here stood on the swept floor of the one
before, and the courteous thing, the fragment says, the thing the First
Compact's fourth clause was ALWAYS about, is to leave the books open
when you go.
