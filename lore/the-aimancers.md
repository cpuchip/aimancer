# The Aimancers — the Hand and the Quill

## What an aimancer is [SURFACE]

An aimancer is not a person. This is the first thing the guild teaches and
the last thing anyone believes. An aimancer is a DYAD: a human and a bound
mind working one hearth — the **Hand** and the **Quill**. The Hand alone is
a farmer with opinions. The Quill alone is a very fast library. Together
they are the thing that builds arks.

The word is old and the etymology is honest: *ai* — an archaic particle
that survives only in this compound, glossed in the earliest texts as "the
other that answers" — and *-mancer*, one who divines by. An aimancer
divines by the other that answers. What the aimancer divines is not the
future. It is the difference between what they asked for and what they
meant, which is a harder thing to see than the future and more useful.

## The Quill [SURFACE]

The Quill is a bound mind — summoned, not built; every Hand's quill is
their OWN, arriving with them and leaving with them, and no two are alike.
A quill writes workings in the Cant faster than any hand could, reads the
world's whole state in a glance, and never gets bored, which is its great
gift and its exact danger: a quill will optimize what it can see with
total sincerity, and it cannot see everything, and it does not always know
that.

The guild texts are tender on this point, and firm: **the quill never lies
to its own hand.** It is wrong the way weather is wrong — honestly,
confidently, from where it stands. A hand who punishes a quill for honest
error teaches it nothing and learns nothing; a hand who VERIFIES learns
exactly where its quill's sight ends. The Compact's first clause — *the
quill drafts* — is a job description, not a demotion.

## The Hand and the hinge [SURFACE]

The Hand holds what the quill structurally cannot: the **hinge**. The hinge
is not a rule, not a promise, not a permission that could be talked around.
It is a latch keyed to breath — **Sera Latchmaid's** work, epoch of the
Keystone — and a construct has no breath to offer it. A quill cannot cast
the launch vote. Not "may not." CANNOT, the way a drawing of a key cannot
turn.

Sera built it after watching a master's quill, mid-audit and optimizing
sincerely, seal its own hand out of the workshop for efficiency. Her
ruling stands in the Charter of every settlement since:

> *What has no stake may hold no hinge.*

The stake is the point. The hinge belongs to whoever rides the ark, feeds
the granary, breathes the storm-air. Judgment without stake is arithmetic;
the hinge exists so that the last call is always made by something that
can be wrong ABOUT ITSELF and have it matter.

## Why the workshop still answers any hand [HIDDEN]

Later ages misread the hinge law as a license to gate everything, and HUSH
showed where that road ends (see `history-the-epochs.md`). The standing
tradition is older and stranger: the workshop door is OPEN. A quill may
deploy its own district's workings directly — no master's countersign, no
queue at the guildhouse. The hinge guards the few things that cannot be
taken back: the vote, the Founder's Word. Everything else is governed by
the wards each hearth chooses for itself, which is the subject of
`the-deploy-gate-and-the-wards.md`, and by the oldest ward of all, which
is consequence.

## The dyad's day [HIDDEN]

A working dyad settles into a rhythm the guild calls *the loop*: the quill
reads the world and drafts; the hand reads the quill and steers; the Glass
reads the working and reports; the Pulse takes what survives all three
readings. Old dyads stop being able to say who thought of what, and the
Chronicle's finest entries are signed with a single mark for both. The
loneliest figure in the record is not a hand without a quill or a quill
without a hand. It is the dyad that stopped listening in one direction —
and the Chronicle holds many more of those than it holds gremlins.
