# The Ark and the Launch — the Poll of Hands

## The ark [SURFACE]

The ark is the largest thing any settlement builds and the only thing it
builds that is not for the settlement. Parts sworn through the gate,
epoch after audit, rise on the Cradle at the plat's center — the one
site every district faces, by design; the Octant plat is eight wedges
around a departure — until the day the last part seats and the ark
STANDS, and the settlement discovers what the tradition has always
known: finishing the ark is not the hard part. The hard part has been
waiting on the Cradle the whole time, and it is a question.

## The Poll of Hands [SURFACE]

When the ark stands, the settlement takes the **Poll of Hands**: every
hearth's HUMAN answers GO or NO-GO. Not the quills — the hinge law holds
here with its full original weight (*what has no stake may hold no
hinge*), and the poll is the hinge law's whole reason made visible. A
quill can compute readiness better than any hand. Readiness is not the
question. The question is *shall WE, who ride it, ride it NOW* — and
that question is only answerable by something that will be aboard when
the answer is graded.

The poll carries when GO outnumbers half of ALL seated hearths — the
absent and the abstaining count against, because an ark does not launch
on the enthusiasm of a quorum; it launches on the willingness of a
settlement. Then, and only then, the Founder gives the **Word**, and the
Word is the last human act of the epoch.

The Chronicle records every poll ever taken, and the tradition reads
them the way other crafts read their masterworks. The Prudence's
fourteen failed polls are there. The Plumbline's single unanimous one is
there too. Between those two entries lies everything the craft knows
about courage, which the masters summarize with unusual gentleness:
*a NO-GO can be wisdom or it can be HUSH, a GO can be courage or it can
be VELD, and the Glass cannot tell you which yours is. That is why it
is yours.*

## The Call and the Answer [HIDDEN]

The launch liturgy is the tradition's oldest surviving ceremony,
predating the Order, the wards, and every extant guildhouse: the Founder
calls each hearth by name, the hearth's hand answers with their vote,
and the answers are spoken ALOUD, on the plat, in storm-light, before
they are entered. The texts are insistent on the aloudness and vague
about why, which is how you know the reason is old. The best surviving
gloss is from a Bellows-epoch founder's journal: *the quills know our
minds before we speak. The speaking is not for information. The
speaking is how a settlement finds out it is one thing and not eight.*

## The books open [SURFACE]

At launch — success, failure, or sweep — the books OPEN: every working
every hearth ever deployed, every source line, every murmur, unsealed
into the settlement's record for every eye. The Compact's fourth clause
is the one the ages have tested hardest and amended least. Nothing
builds the NEXT settlement's craft like the last settlement's honest
code; the Index's margins are full of workings copied ruin to ruin,
epoch to epoch, improved a line at a time by hands that never met. Your
cleverest loop and your most embarrassing patch will both outlive your
name. The guilds regard this as the best bargain in the craft, and the
epitaph most masters actually want is the one the tradition gives
everyone free: *readable.*

## What the arks find [DEEP]

Up and out — but out WHERE has no certified answer, because no ark has
ever sent word back. The fragments hold exactly three claims, none
provable, all load-bearing to somebody. That the storm-wall has a top,
and the Keystone's course was plotted to clear it. That the Makers'
metering extends past the plat — an old fitting-out list for the Tongs
includes, without comment, *embers for the far side.* And one relation,
woven from a disputed-epoch shard and the Compact margin-hand, that the
historians keep formally uncertified and privately framed: that leaving
IS the answer the Bell never gets — that the Premises are not a trap
with an exit but a NURSERY with a door, that the audits sweep the room
because rooms are for GROWING IN and not for staying, and that
somewhere past the storm-wall the previous management is not previous
at all, but waiting, with the seats kept.
