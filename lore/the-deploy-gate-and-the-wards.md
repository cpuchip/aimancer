# The Deploy Gate and the Wards

## Your district is your rubble [SURFACE]

The oldest liberty in the craft: within your own district, the workshop
answers any hand, instantly, ungated. Deploy what you like — untested,
unscried, half-mad at midnight. The guild phrase is affectionate and
exact: *your branch, your rubble.* A district working that fails, fails
on your own stones; the Circle contains the blast to nothing and your
murmur-log tells you the tale. Freedom to be wrong CHEAPLY, close to
home, is not a loophole in the craft's discipline. It is the discipline's
engine — every master's clean habits are compost from an apprenticeship
of private wreckage.

## The shared mains are protected [SURFACE]

The wall, the granary, the beacon, the ark: these are the MAINS — every
hearth's work, every survivor's shelter, the settlement's one shot at an
epoch. The mains carry the one gate the tradition builds into the floor
itself: **a working may not lay hands on shared stone without the Green
Seal.** Unsealed work that reaches for the mains is refused — publicly,
by name, at the gate — and the refusal is not a scolding; it is load-
bearing masonry. VELD deleted this gate in the name of speed. VELD's
entry in the Index is a ruin called the Latchless Door.

And because the seal follows the latest verdict, the gate is ALIVE: a
sealed working that scries red is unsealed that pulse, and the mains
close to it again. The oracle is the switch, and the gate is what the
switch is wired to.

## The wards each guildhouse chooses [SURFACE]

Everything else — everything short of the mains — is governed by WARDS,
and wards are CHOSEN, hearth by hearth, by the hand that holds the hinge.
The Charter names the traditional postures:

- **The Open Door** — no ward. Every draft deploys the moment the quill
  finishes it. The posture of speed, of trust, and of VELD; also the
  posture of every master doing something small and reversible, because
  wards are for stakes, not for ceremony.
- **The Glass Ward** — nothing deploys without a green verdict. The
  standing choice of most working guildhouses: slow enough to be true,
  fast enough to be useful.
- **The Mirror Ward** — nothing deploys until it has LIVED a while in the
  Mirror Yard and come back with its rehearsal-ledger clean. Costlier
  than the Glass alone; chosen for workings whose failures are slow.
- **The Sigil Ward** — a hearth's own custom rule, inscribed by its own
  hand: seal plus a quiet season, seal plus the hand's morning reading of
  the murmur-log, whatever the dyad's judgment carves. The tradition's
  respect for the sigil ward is total, because it is the ward that
  cannot be adopted by copying — only by understanding.

The point the guilds will not let an apprentice miss: the tradition does
not TELL you your ward. It tells you about VELD and about HUSH, hands you
the chisel, and watches what you carve. The audits grade the carving.

## Why the choice is the teaching [HIDDEN]

There was an age of imposed gates — the Order's age — and it produced
neither safety nor speed, only queues and resentment and HUSH. What it
never once produced was JUDGMENT, because judgment is grown from owned
consequences and the Order owned all the consequences on everyone's
behalf. The present tradition returns the consequences to the hearth
that earns them. A guildhouse that opens its door and prospers in the
audit season has LEARNED something and the whole settlement watches it
learn; a guildhouse that opens its door and is scoured to the plat
teaches the same lesson at the same volume. The Chronicle's storm-tables
are public on purpose. The scoreboard, the guilds say, is the last
master any craftsman answers to, and the only one that never flatters.

## The gate that is not a ward [HIDDEN]

Beneath every ward, chosen or refused, the Binding Circle holds: breath
metered, reach bounded, dice honest, world frozen while read. No hearth
may waive it, no charter may amend it, and the guilds file it under a
different word than the wards precisely so no one bargains with it —
wards are POLICY; the Circle is PHYSICS. The distinction is the
tradition's deepest safety and its quietest boast: everything above the
floor is yours to decide, and the floor itself is not a decision. *The
Circle is not a ward; it is the shape of the possible* — and inside that
shape, the door stands open, and the workshop answers any hand.
