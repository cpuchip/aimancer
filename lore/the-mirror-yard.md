# The Mirror Yard — where spells rehearse

## The shadow settlement [SURFACE]

Stand at the Glass and ask for more than a verdict — ask for TIME — and
the Glass will cast you the **Mirror Yard**: a whole shadow of the
settlement as it stands this pulse, wall for wall, vein for vein, storm-
countdown and all, forked off the real and run FORWARD, pulse after
rehearsed pulse, with your working living inside it. Watch it mine the
mirrored veins. Watch it starve, or flourish, or do the subtly wrong
thing on the fortieth pulse that no single-glance verdict would ever
catch. Then the rehearsal ends, the Yard folds, and nothing — nothing —
follows you back except the ledger of what happened.

The Yard is the craft's answer to the oldest gap in verification: a
green seal proves a working DOESN'T BREAK. Only the Yard shows what it
DOES.

## The Yard forgets by design [HIDDEN]

Nothing done in the Yard is real. Ore mined there enriches no one; walls
raised there shelter no one; a catastrophe there scorches nothing but
your expectations. The Yard's forgetting is not a limitation the guilds
tolerate — it is the entire point, guaranteed by the same physics as the
Glass itself: the Yard is a rehearsal-world animated on borrowed
attention, and when the embers stop, it is not destroyed. It simply
never was.

The masters put it to apprentices as the Yard's one law, and they put it
warmly: *the Yard is the only place in the world where you may be
completely wrong for free. Spend generously.*

## Private by tradition [HIDDEN]

A Yard rehearsal reports to the hearth that paid for it and to no one
else. Your neighbors see neither your rehearsed triumphs nor your
rehearsed disasters, and the Chronicle takes no record of Yard outcomes
at all. The reasoning is old and humane: rehearsal is where the craft
does its honest fumbling, and fumbling watched is fumbling stopped. The
dyads that grow fastest are the ones whose Yards would be the most
embarrassing to publish. The tradition protects that embarrassment like
a seedbed — with one wry exception. At launch, when the books open, more
than one master has voluntarily appended their Yard-ledgers to the
record, disasters and all. The Chronicle files these under a heading the
first such master chose herself: *how it actually went.*

## What the Yard is a piece of [DEEP]

The Glass rehearses a working; the Yard rehearses a WORLD; and the
Premises — bounded, furnished, metered, swept and reset on schedule —
rehearse something no fragment has ever named. The relation is there for
anyone who lines the three up, and the historians decline to certify it
in either direction, because both directions are load-bearing and only
one can be true. Either the Yard is a small copy of the world's own
nature, a mirror of a mirror, rehearsals all the way up — or the world
is the ORIGINAL, the one performance, and the audits are simply what it
costs to keep a stage. The oldest marginal note on the question is also
the only comforting one, unsigned, in the same hand as the Compact's
margin: *if this is the rehearsal, then somewhere there is an opening
night, and they have kept the seats.*
