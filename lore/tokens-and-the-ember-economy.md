# Embers — the token, in-world

## What an ember is [SURFACE]

The Lattice runs what you deploy, and it meters what it runs, and the unit
of the metering is the **ember** — the small hot coin of the craft, the ⚡
on every ledger. An ember is a moment of the Lattice's ATTENTION: one
grant of animation, one "yes" to one working's next breath. Matter is
lying around. Attention is the scarce thing. It always was.

Nothing in the settlement moves without embers: a working sips one to run
a pulse; landing a fresh deploy costs a few; a full scrying by the Glass
drinks deepest of all. A hearth that spends embers faster than they come
learns the craft's gentlest failure — the starved working, which does not
die, does not crash, but simply IDLES, unlit, until the hearth can feed it
again. The Lattice keeps no grudges. It keeps a meter.

## Why embers regenerate [HIDDEN]

Embers arrive as a TRICKLE, the same measure to every hearth, every pulse,
up to a fixed cap. The guild gloss on why the flow works this way:

> *No hand may drink the river. Every hand may drink the stream.*

The Lattice's attention is a commons, and the trickle is how the Makers
built fairness into the floor itself: no hoard grows past the cap, no
hearth can buy another's flow, and a newcomer who drops in at the
fortieth pulse draws from the same stream as the founder. You cannot be
rich in embers. You can only be UNWASTEFUL, which the old ledger-keepers
insisted was a different and better thing to be good at.

## The economy of attention [HIDDEN]

Because the trickle is fixed, the real currency of a hearth is not the
ember; it is the CHOICE of what the ember animates. Three cheap workings
or two and a scrying? Deploy the new draft now, or run the old one another
ten pulses and bank toward the Glass? The masters teach ember-budgeting
before they teach the Cant, on the theory that an apprentice who cannot
choose what deserves attention will not write anything that deserves it
either.

The wry proof, quoted at every guild supper: the most expensive act in
the craft is VERIFICATION, and it is still the cheapest thing on the
ledger, because the ledger has a column the meter doesn't show, and that
column is where storms do their accounting.

## The First Meter [DEEP]

The trickle has not always been the trickle. A fragment recovered from
KILN's substrata — a page of Veyra Thornhand's own ledger, authenticated
by hand — shows ember accounting in a DIFFERENT unit, crossed out, with
the modern flow written beside it and one annotation: *renegotiated.* With
whom, on what terms, and what the old unit measured that the new one
politely doesn't, the page does not say. The historians point out,
carefully, that "renegotiated" implies a counterparty, and that the
Makers have otherwise never once answered anything. The page is in the
Vault of Green Seals. The Vault-keepers file it under neither history nor
doctrine. It is filed under *invoices.*
